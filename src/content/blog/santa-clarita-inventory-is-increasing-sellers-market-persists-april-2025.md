## Glossary of Key Terms

	- **Inventory (Real Estate):** The total number of properties available for sale in a specific market at a given time.

	- **Buyer Drive:** The level of demand from individuals looking to purchase real estate in a particular market.

	- **Seller's Market:** A real estate market condition characterized by high demand from buyers and a limited supply of homes for sale, typically resulting in higher prices and quicker sales.

	- **Mortgage Interest Deduction:** A provision in tax law that allows homeowners to deduct the interest paid on their mortgage loan, potentially reducing their overall tax liability.

	- **CPA (Certified Public Accountant):** A professional who has met specific educational and licensing requirements and is qualified to provide accounting and tax advice.

	- **Market Collapsing:** A sudden and significant decline in real estate prices, often associated with economic downturns or financial crises.

	- **Lender:** A financial institution or individual that provides loans, in this context, for purchasing real estate.

	- **Creditworthiness:** An assessment of an individual's ability and willingness to repay debts, often based on their credit history.

	- **FICO Score:** A type of credit score used by lenders to assess credit risk.

	- **Debt-to-Income Ratio:** A personal finance measure that compares an individual's monthly debt payments to their gross monthly income.

	- **Sitemap:** A file that lists the pages of a website in order to help search engines like Google understand the site's structure.

	- **Priority Level (Sitemap):** A value assigned to URLs in a sitemap to indicate their importance relative to other URLs on the site for search engine crawling. A value between 0.0 and 1.0 is used.

	- **Market Report:** An analysis of recent trends and data in a specific real estate market, often including information on sales volume, prices, and inventory.

	- **Social Media Platform:** A website or application that enables users to create and share content or to participate in social networking.
