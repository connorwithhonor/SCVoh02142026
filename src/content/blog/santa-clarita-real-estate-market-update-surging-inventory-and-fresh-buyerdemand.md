Days on Market (DOM) is a metric that indicates how quickly homes are selling. When DOM is low, it typically suggests high demand and/or low inventory, meaning sellers can ask for premium prices. When DOM begins to rise, it could indicate either an increase in inventory, a slowdown in buyer activity, or both. In Santa Clarita, a gradually rising DOM can be attributed partly to the fact that more homes are becoming available. While this may seem less advantageous for sellers, it does not necessarily signal a weak market‚Äîit simply underscores the importance of competitive pricing and quality marketing.

Buyers can take DOM data into consideration when deciding on an offer price. If a property has been on the market longer than the local average, a buyer may attempt to negotiate more aggressively.
