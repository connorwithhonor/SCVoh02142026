## **How to Maximize Your Open House Visit**

When you attend an open house, **knowing what to look for** can make the difference between **finding a great deal** and **making a costly mistake**.

### **1. Look Beyond Aesthetics**

Many homes are **staged** to look their best, but you should focus on **structural integrity and functionality** rather than just d√©cor.

	- **Inspect the Roof** ‚Äì Look for **missing tiles, cracks, or sagging sections**.

	- **Examine the Windows and Doors** ‚Äì Ensure they **open and close properly**.

	- **Check for Water Damage** ‚Äì Look for **stains on ceilings or near baseboards**, which could indicate **past leaks**.

### **2. Pay Attention to the HVAC System**

Heating and cooling systems are **some of the most expensive home components to replace**.

	- **Look at the outdoor AC unit** ‚Äì If it appears **rusted or outdated**, it might need replacing.

	- **Check air vents** ‚Äì Dirty vents may indicate **a neglected HVAC system**.

### **3. Observe the Neighborhood**

The **surrounding environment** is just as important as the home itself.

	- **Evaluate Street Traffic** ‚Äì A quiet neighborhood during an open house might be **loud and busy** in the evenings.

	- **Check for Nearby Amenities** ‚Äì Consider **proximity to grocery stores, restaurants, and schools**.

	- **Look at Neighboring Properties** ‚Äì Poorly maintained homes nearby could **lower property values**.
