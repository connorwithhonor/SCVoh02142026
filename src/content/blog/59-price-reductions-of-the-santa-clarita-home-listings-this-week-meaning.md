## **The Home Inspection Process: What to Expect**

A home inspection is your chance to **assess the condition of the property** before finalizing the purchase. It ensures that you're making a sound investment and helps you avoid unexpected issues down the road.

### **1. Scheduling the Home Inspection**

Once your offer is accepted, schedule a home inspection **within the contingency period** (typically 5-7 days). Your real estate agent can recommend a trusted inspector, or you can choose your own.

A **certified home inspector** will assess the home‚Äôs:

	- 
	**Foundation and Structure** ‚Äì Checking for cracks, settling, or shifting

	

	- 
	**Roof and Exterior** ‚Äì Looking for leaks, damage, or wear

	

	- 
	**Plumbing System** ‚Äì Testing water pressure and checking for leaks

	

	- 
	**Electrical System** ‚Äì Ensuring safe wiring and proper grounding

	

	- 
	**HVAC System** ‚Äì Evaluating heating and cooling efficiency

	

	- 
	**Appliances** ‚Äì Ensuring they function properly

	

Find Santa Clarita homes with **the best features and conditions** by using our [Advanced Home Search Tool](https://www.santaclaritaopenhouses.com/advanced-search).

### **2. Reviewing the Home Inspection Report**

Once the inspection is complete, the inspector will provide a **detailed report** outlining any issues. The findings typically fall into three categories:

	- 
	**Minor Issues** ‚Äì Cosmetic or small repairs

	

	- 
	**Moderate Concerns** ‚Äì Things that should be fixed but aren‚Äôt urgent

	

	- 
	**Major Problems** ‚Äì Structural, electrical, plumbing, or safety issues

	

If major concerns arise, you may need to **renegotiate** or ask the seller to cover repairs. Your real estate agent will help you navigate this step.

## **Understanding Contingencies and Negotiation Strategies**

### **1. The Key Contingencies in a Home Purchase**

Contingencies are conditions in the contract that **protect the buyer** and allow you to back out if certain criteria aren‚Äôt met. The most common contingencies include:

#### **A. Inspection Contingency**

Gives you time to **review the home inspection report** and request repairs or credits. If major issues arise, you can cancel the contract without penalty.

#### **B. Appraisal Contingency**

Ensures the home‚Äôs value aligns with the **lender‚Äôs appraisal.** If the appraisal comes in lower than the purchase price, you can renegotiate or back out.

Check out current [Santa Clarita Home Prices](https://www.santaclaritaopenhouses.com/santa-clarita-market-report) to compare appraisal values.

#### **C. Financing Contingency**

Allows you to cancel if you‚Äôre unable to secure financing. This is why **getting pre-approved** (or better yet, fully underwritten) is crucial before making an offer. Need help with financing? Check out [Santa Clarita Mortgage Rates](https://www.santaclaritaopenhouses.com/mortgage-rates).

#### **D. Title and HOA Contingencies**

Protects you from issues like undisclosed liens, property boundary disputes, or unexpected **homeowners‚Äô association (HOA) fees.**

### **2. Negotiating Repairs and Seller Credits**

If the inspection uncovers significant issues, you have a few options:

	- 
	Request the seller **make repairs** before closing

	

	- 
	Ask for a **credit** at closing to cover the costs

	

	- 
	Negotiate a **price reduction**

	

Your real estate agent will help craft a strong **repair request** while keeping the deal moving forward.

## **The Escrow Process: What Happens Next?**

Once contingencies are met, you‚Äôll move into **escrow**, which is the final step before closing on your home. Escrow is a neutral third-party service that ensures both parties meet contractual obligations before funds are released.

### **1. Opening Escrow**

After signing the contract, a **title company or escrow officer** will open an account and hold your deposit (**earnest money**). This deposit is typically **1-3% of the home‚Äôs purchase price.**

### **2. Home Appraisal and Loan Approval**

	- 
	The lender orders an **appraisal** to confirm the home‚Äôs value.

	

	- 
	Underwriters **review your financial documents** before issuing a **final loan approval.**

	

	- 
	If everything checks out, you‚Äôll receive a **Closing Disclosure** outlining final terms and costs.

	

### **3. Homeowners Insurance and Final Walkthrough**

Before closing, you‚Äôll need:

	- 
	**Homeowners insurance** to protect your investment

	

	- 
	A **final walkthrough** to ensure repairs are completed and the home is in expected condition

On closing day, you‚Äôll sign all final documents, including:

	- 
	Mortgage agreements

	

	- 
	Title and ownership documents

	

	-

Once signed, the lender will **fund your loan**, the escrow company will **transfer ownership**, and you‚Äôll receive the **keys to your new home!**
