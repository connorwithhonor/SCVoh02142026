## Section 5: Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### What are USDA Loans and Who Qualifies?

USDA loans are government-backed loans designed to help low-to-moderate income buyers purchase homes in designated rural and suburban areas. To qualify, buyers typically need a credit score of at least 600, meet income limits specific to the area, and purchase property in an eligible location.

### How Do Zero Down Payment Loans Work?

Zero down payment loans, such as USDA and VA loans, finance 100% of the home‚Äôs purchase price. This means buyers do not need to provide a traditional down payment, though there may be additional fees or mortgage insurance requirements involved.

The USDA loan process is similar to other mortgage types. After pre-qualification and documentation, underwriting can take anywhere from 30 to 60 days. For manufactured home or land purchases with construction, you may have up to 11 months post-closing to complete the building process.

### Can USDA Loans Be Used for Home Renovations?

While USDA loans are primarily used for purchasing properties, some programs allow for additional financing to cover home improvements or renovations, especially if the property meets the necessary criteria.
