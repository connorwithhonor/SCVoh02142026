## Seller Consultations and Valuation Insights

During a recent consultation on September 21, 2025, two sellers expressed reluctance to invest further in their property to prepare it for sale. Their primary concern was avoiding additional costs, with a secondary question about what improvements might be necessary. From a valuation perspective, several factors are critical:

	- **Comparable Sales (Comps):** Appraisers typically use properties sold within the past 180 days as a benchmark, though closer timeframes (e.g., the current month) are preferable for accuracy. This depends on market conditions and the cost of bringing in comps, but 180 days is a standard rule of thumb. Properties beyond this period may be considered only with extenuating circumstances, such as unique market shifts.

	- **Proximity and Similarity:** Comps should be in the same neighborhood or nearby, closed recently, and similar in year built, condition, and upgrades. For example, if comparable properties have undergone copper repiping or roof replacements while the subject property retains original features, this impacts valuation. Buyers will compare these factors when making offers.

	- **Current Market Advantage:** With 726 listings marking a six-month low, competition is reduced. For the consulted sellers, their property‚Äôs condition aligns with available comps, suggesting no immediate need for upgrades. This lack of direct competition supports listing in its current state.
