## The Evolution of Real Estate Commissions &amp; Why It Matters

The real estate industry is undergoing major changes, particularly when it comes to **commissions and agent representation.** A recent lawsuit against the National Association of Realtors (NAR) has forced real estate professionals to be more transparent about commission structures and the value they bring to their clients.

For years, buyers and sellers have often assumed that commissions were fixed. However, this lawsuit has clarified an important fact: **commissions are negotiable, and agents must prove their worth.**

    - **Sellers** are questioning traditional commission rates and want more transparency in pricing.

    - **Buyers** now realize they may need to directly compensate their agent, making them more selective in hiring.

    - **Agents** who fail to communicate their value effectively are losing business.

## Why Buyers &amp; Sellers Need to Be More Selective With Agents

With this shift in the market, buyers and sellers can no longer afford to hire agents based on tradition alone. They need real professionals who:

    - Understand **negotiation strategies** that benefit their clients.

    - Have a proven **track record of closing deals** successfully.

    - Use advanced **marketing and advertising** techniques to maximize exposure.

### Questions Buyers Should Ask Their Agent

If you're in the market for a home, make sure your agent can answer these questions:

    - How will you **negotiate the best deal** for me?

    - What do you know about **market trends** and pricing?

    - What steps do you take to **protect me** from bad deals?

If your agent *cannot clearly answer these questions*, they may not be the right fit.

Today‚Äôs buyers and sellers have access to more real estate data than ever before. A quick search can reveal:

Real estate negotiations aren‚Äôt just about price. **Inspection issues, closing costs, and contingencies** all play a role. An experienced agent can save you money and protect your interests by negotiating:

    - **Better contract terms** that prevent costly mistakes

Sellers who rely solely on listing their home on Zillow are missing out. **Top-tier real estate agents offer professional marketing, targeted advertising, and direct buyer outreach.**

For example, here‚Äôs what a true expert does to maximize exposure:

# How Interest Rates Are Shaping Santa Clarita Real Estate in 2025

## Watch Before You Read

Interest rates are affecting home prices, affordability, and the overall market. Watch the video below to understand why now is the time for buyers and sellers to make their move.

## Why Interest Rates Matter More Than Ever

Mortgage interest rates are one of the most significant factors in real estate. They directly impact **home affordability, buyer demand, and overall market activity**. As we move through 2025, Santa Clarita‚Äôs real estate market is being shaped by interest rate trends that will determine whether prices rise, fall, or stabilize.

In this post, we‚Äôll cover:

    - How **higher interest rates** have affected buyers and sellers

    - Why rates are expected to **change** in 2025

    - The best strategies for **buyers and sellers to capitalize** on the current market

## How Rising Interest Rates Have Affected Santa Clarita Real Estate

Over the past few years, we‚Äôve seen significant fluctuations in interest rates. In 2021 and 2022, mortgage rates were at **historic lows**, fueling one of the hottest real estate markets in history. However, as inflation concerns grew, the Federal Reserve raised rates aggressively in 2023 and 2024.

As a result:

    - Monthly mortgage payments **increased**, making homes less affordable.

    - Buyer demand **dropped** as purchasing power weakened.

    - Homes took **longer to sell** due to affordability concerns.

Despite these challenges, Santa Clarita real estate remains strong because demand for housing is still high, and inventory levels have remained relatively stable.

## What‚Äôs Expected for Interest Rates in 2025?

Experts predict that mortgage rates will **gradually decrease** throughout 2025 as inflation stabilizes. If that happens, we can expect:

    - More **buyers entering the market** as affordability improves.

    - Home prices to **rise again** due to increased competition.

    - Sellers to have **greater negotiating power** as demand increases.

## What Should Buyers Do Right Now?

If you‚Äôre a buyer, you may be wondering: **Should I wait for interest rates to drop?** While waiting may seem like a good idea, it could actually **cost you more in the long run**. Here‚Äôs why:

### 1. Home Prices Will Likely Rise

As soon as rates drop, more buyers will flood the market. Increased demand will **drive home prices higher**, meaning the savings from a lower interest rate could be wiped out by a higher purchase price.

### 2. Less Competition Right Now

With higher rates, fewer buyers are in the market. That means you **face less competition**, giving you more negotiating power. You may be able to secure a home **below asking price** or with seller concessions.

### 3. You Can Refinance Later

If you buy now and rates drop in the future, you always have the option to **refinance to a lower rate**. This allows you to secure a great home today while still benefiting from future rate cuts.
