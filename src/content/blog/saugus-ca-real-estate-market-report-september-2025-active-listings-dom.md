# Section 1: Active Listings in Saugus ‚Äì 2021 to 2025

The first key metric is **active listings** ‚Äî the number of homes available for sale at a given time. This figure reflects supply, and when paired with buyer demand, tells us whether the market favors sellers or buyers.

	- 
	**January 2021:** Saugus started with **100 active listings**. Homes were moving rapidly, and inventory was tight.

	

	- 
	**Summer 2022:** Listings surged above **160 homes** mid-year, reflecting a cooling market as mortgage rates began to rise.

	

	- 
	**2023:** Inventory fluctuated dramatically, dropping to just **97 homes in June 2023**, before rising again toward the end of the year.

	

	- 
	**2024:** Steady climbs returned, with monthly averages between **130 and 158 homes**, creating a more balanced environment.

	

	- 
	**2025:** By mid-2025, Saugus hit its highest inventory levels in four years, with **214 homes in July 2025**. Even August 2025, which typically sees a seasonal slowdown, still showed **184 homes** active on the market.

	

This climb is significant. From early 2021 to mid-2025, **inventory nearly doubled**. For buyers, that means more options. For sellers, it signals more competition ‚Äî staging, pricing strategy, and presentation are now critical.

# Section 2: Days to Sell ‚Äì Median Time on Market

The second critical metric is **median days to sell**. This reflects how quickly homes go from active to sold.

	- 
	**2021‚Äì2022:** Homes often sold in less than **10 days**. Multiple offers and over-asking bids were common, reflecting a hyper-competitive market.

	

	- 
	**2023:** Median days began creeping up, ranging from **15‚Äì20 days** as affordability challenges limited buyer urgency.

	

	- 
	**2024:** The trend solidified ‚Äî homes averaged **20‚Äì25 days on market**. Some stayed even longer if overpriced.

	

	- 
	**2025:** By mid-2025, homes were typically taking **25‚Äì30 days** to sell. That‚Äôs still healthy compared to historic averages, but it represents a clear shift from the frenzy of 2021.

	

This increase in DOM shows that **buyers have more breathing room**. They can tour multiple properties, negotiate terms, and make more thoughtful decisions. Sellers must adapt by being competitive ‚Äî pricing realistically, improving curb appeal, and offering attractive buyer incentives when appropriate.

# Section 3: Seasonal Trends in Saugus

The data also highlights **seasonal patterns** in the Saugus market:

	- 
	**Spring (March‚ÄìMay):** Consistently shows the highest number of new listings. For example, May 2025 reached **205 homes**, the highest of the year. Buyers benefit from the largest selection, but competition is also strongest.

	

	- 
	**Summer (June‚ÄìAugust):** Inventory peaks, but demand often dips as families travel. Sellers may need to adjust expectations ‚Äî June 2023 saw just **97 active homes**, but by July 2025, that number more than doubled to **214**.

	

	- 
	**Fall/Winter (September‚ÄìDecember):** Traditionally slower, but still active. Motivated sellers list, and serious buyers secure deals. December 2021 had only **89 active listings**, compared to **143 in December 2024**.

	

These cycles matter when timing your move. Listing in spring can maximize exposure, while shopping in late summer or winter may present the best negotiating leverage.
