## What Are Assumable Mortgages?

An assumable mortgage is a home loan that a buyer can take over from the seller, inheriting the existing terms, including the interest rate, remaining balance, and repayment period. Instead of applying for a new mortgage at current market rates, the buyer ‚Äúassumes‚Äù the seller‚Äôs loan, potentially locking in a lower rate from years past. Assumable mortgages aren‚Äôt as common as they once were. Most conventional loans today are not assumable without lender approval, and even when allowed, the process can be complex. However, certain government-backed loans‚Äîsuch as VA, FHA, and USDA loans‚Äîoften include assumable provisions, though they come with specific eligibility requirements. For example, VA loans may require the buyer to be a qualified veteran or meet credit standards, while FHA loans might need lender approval.
