	- **Move forward**: If you accept the compromise, remove contingencies and proceed to loan docs. Stay vigilant about lender requests and document accuracy.

The seller digs in their heels and refuses all repairs. This is where things get spicy. You‚Äôve got two choices:

	- **Walk away**: If you‚Äôre still within your contingency period, you can cancel without losing your deposit. Sellers aren‚Äôt just being stubborn‚Äîthey‚Äôre weighing risks too. If they deny your repairs and you walk, they‚Äôre back to square one, facing:

## The Contingent Offer Trap: When Sellers Are Also Buyers

If your seller is buying another home, your deal might be part of a chain of contingent transactions. This is common in Santa Clarita, where sellers often need to sell to buy. Here‚Äôs what you need to know:

	- **Contingent deals are risky**: If the seller‚Äôs purchase falls through, your deal could collapse like a house of cards. Your agent should verify the status of the seller‚Äôs sale‚Äîask if they‚Äôre past the contingency removal point (see [https://www.santaclaritaopenhouses.com/considering-offers](https://www.santaclaritaopenhouses.com/considering-offers)).

	- **Non-contingent offers win**: Cash buyers or non-contingent offers often trump contingent ones. If you‚Äôre in a contingent deal, strengthen your offer with a higher price or flexible terms.

	- **Communication is key**: Your agent should talk to the seller‚Äôs agent (with permission) to gauge the strength of the seller‚Äôs sale. This avoids surprises later.
