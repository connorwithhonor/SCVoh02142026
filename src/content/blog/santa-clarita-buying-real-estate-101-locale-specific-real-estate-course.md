**Current Market Status**

The number of active listings and the absorption rate are key indicators of whether we are in a buyer's or seller's market. For many years, Santa Clarita has been in a seller's market, starting from the market bottom in 2011-2012 after the foreclosure inventory was absorbed. This trend has continued, with the market performing better than before.

**What to Expect**

In the coming months, we can expect fluctuations in inventory levels, but the overall trend suggests a robust market. Sellers can leverage the low inventory to command better prices, while buyers should be prepared to act quickly when they find the right property. Staying informed about these trends will help you make strategic decisions.

**Professional Guidance**

Seeking out a reliable real estate agent is crucial. Look for someone with local market knowledge and experience. As someone who has been selling houses since 1998, I emphasize the importance of professional guidance in navigating the complexities of the market.

**Real Estate Buyer Training: First to Communications**

Preparing to buy a home involves several important steps. Here are the foundational steps every buyer should consider:

**Self-Assessment**

Before embarking on the journey of homeownership, it's important to evaluate your personal situation. Ask yourself whether buying real estate makes sense for your long-term goals. Consider your financial stability and whether the property will serve as a primary residence or an investment.

**Purpose and Timing**

Clarify the purpose of your purchase. If it's going to be your primary residence, it probably makes sense to proceed. Timing is also crucial; understanding market conditions and trends can significantly impact your buying decision.

**Financial Qualification**

Determining how much you can afford to spend on a home is essential. Consult with trusted lenders and mortgage brokers to assess your financial situation and pre-qualify for a loan. This step will help you understand your purchasing power and set realistic expectations.

**Building Your Team**

Assemble a team of trusted professionals, including real estate agents, lenders, inspectors, and possibly real estate attorneys. These experts will play a critical role in ensuring a smooth and successful transaction. Choose professionals who are well-regarded and have a proven track record of reliability and integrity.

**Personal Insights and Tips**

Throughout my career, I've learned that practical tips and personal anecdotes can greatly enhance your understanding of the real estate market. Here are a few insights from my experience:

	- **Patience and Preparation**: The real estate market can be unpredictable. Patience and thorough preparation are key to making sound decisions.

	- **Market Knowledge**: Stay informed about market trends and conditions. This knowledge will give you an edge in negotiations and decision-making.

	- **Professional Support**: Leverage the expertise of your real estate team. Their insights and guidance are invaluable in navigating the complexities of the market.
